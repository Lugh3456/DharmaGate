心

Mahayana · 大乘

般若波罗蜜多心经 Heart Sutra

The most widely recited Buddhist text in the world. In 260 Chinese characters, it reveals the doctrine of emptiness — "form is emptiness, emptiness is form" — chanted daily in temples across East Asia.

空 Emptiness Prajnaparamita 260 Characters

金

Mahayana · 大乘

金刚般若波罗蜜经 Diamond Sutra

A dialogue between the Buddha and his disciple Subhuti on non-attachment and the perfection of wisdom. Central to Zen/Chan Buddhism — the world's oldest printed book carries its text.

无执 Non-attachment 禅 Zen/Chan Wisdom

莲

Mahayana · 大乘

妙法莲华经 Lotus Sutra

Teaches that all beings, without exception, can attain Buddhahood. The most important sutra in China and Japan — foundational to Tiantai, Tendai, and Nichiren Buddhism.

佛性 Buddha-nature 一乘 One Vehicle 天台宗

华

Mahayana · 大乘

大方广佛华严经 Avatamsaka Sutra

The grandest of all Buddhist sutras. Describes the universe as an infinite web of interdependence — illustrated by Indra's Net, where every jewel reflects all others. Foundational to Huayan Buddhism.

因陀罗网 Indra's Net 华严宗 Interdependence

维摩诘所说经 Vimalakirti Sutra

A wealthy layman outshines all of the Buddha's monk disciples in wisdom. Famous for Vimalakirti's "thunderous silence" when asked about non-duality — enlightenment is open to everyone, not just monastics.

Mahayana · 大乘 (Pure Land)

阿弥陀经 Amitabha Sutra

Describes the Western Pure Land of Amitabha Buddha — a paradise where all beings may be reborn and easily achieve enlightenment. Core text of Pure Land Buddhism, the most widely practised Buddhist school in East Asia.

法句经 Dhammapada

423 verses of the Buddha's wisdom, organised into 26 themes. The most widely read Theravada text — accessible, direct, and timeless. Opens with: "Mind is the forerunner of all actions."

经集 Sutta Nipata

One of the oldest Buddhist texts. A collection of discourses in verse and prose on ethics, renunciation, and wisdom. Contains the beloved Metta Sutta — the Buddha's teaching on loving-kindness toward all beings.

中阴得度 Bardo Thodol

The Tibetan Book of the Dead. A guide read aloud to the dying and recently deceased, describing the journey of consciousness through the bardo — the intermediate state between death and rebirth. Liberation lies in recognising the nature of mind.

About This Collection

The Dharma Gate brings together nine of Buddhism's most essential scriptures — spanning the Theravada, Mahayana, and Vajrayana traditions. Each text is presented with its original Chinese name, key teachings, and a study guide — as a personal companion for reading, reflection, and practice.
